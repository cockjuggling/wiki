# Princes Street fire at the old Debenhams Department Store

## Summary

A major fire broke out in the early hours of Thursday, 9 July 2026, in the former Debenhams department store at 101–109 Princes Street, Edinburgh, with the Scottish Fire and Rescue Service alerted at around 2:52am and mobilising roughly 50 firefighters, six fire appliances and two high-reach appliances to bring it under control. Crews contained the blaze to the affected site with no reported casualties, though the roof and top floors of the disused building were destroyed. The building has remained cordoned off since the blaze, as engineers work to assess its stability.  A joint investigation by Police Scotland and the Fire and Rescue Service into the cause is still ongoing. As a result, the section of Princes Street between Frederick Street and South Charlotte Street has remained closed to traffic, with bus and tram diversions in place Disruption is expected to continue into August, overlapping with the Edinburgh Fringe.
